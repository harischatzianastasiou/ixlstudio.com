# GAME DESIGN DOCUMENT

## 1. Core Player Fantasy
The player is sent from the far future to live inside the mind of a citizen of a future tech society that is sunk in numbness.
The goal is to slowly rewrite the narrative that the citizen has for the world, and thus "wake them up".

## 2. Scenario

## 2.1 The Transition (Pre-2100 to 2100)
By 2100, artificial intelligence and automation spread across every major sector of society: finance, logistics, design, marketing, software, and manufacturing. 
At first, automation replaces factory labor. Soon after, office jobs decline sharply, followed by disruptions in logistics and education. 

A small number of positions remain, occupied by individuals responsible for supervising AI systems.

As employment collapses, wealth accelerates upward. The wealthy no longer represent successful individuals, but they become system owners. Those who control AI infrastructure, machine labor, and data rights rise above traditional governments. Governments attempt regulation but fail, as economic power moves faster than political authority. In this scenery, cities restructure. The old middle class is pushed outward, unable to keep up with the new economic reality. A new middle class forms, small tech founders, influencers, AI operators, storekeepers, and already-wealthy individuals who can interface with the systems.

No one stops the transition:
• The rich are comfortable.
• The middle class shrinks and fails to unite.
• The poor have no leverage.

### 2.2 The Rise of Elite Cities  (2100)
Mega-corporations transform major urban centers into elite smart cities optimized for efficiency, safety, and productivity.

These cities deploy:
• Automated policing  
• Smart transportation  
• Predictive governance  
• Robotic services  
• AI personal assistants  

Life inside is clean, safe, efficient and extremely expensive.

The cities are built for:
• Corporate leadership  
• Political elites  
• Investors  
• High-value specialists  
• Cultural influencers

The **Identity Chip** is introduced.

### 2.3 The Identity Chip

The chip replaces:
• Legal identity  
• Banking and payments  
• Transportation access  
• Internet connectivity  
• Security clearance  
• Healthcare priority  
• Housing eligibility  
• Education pathways  

The chip is mandatory for residency. If you have it, you belong. If you don’t, you are invisible.

Every interaction between citizens triggers a passive exchange of Identity Chip data.
Basic status markers - compliance level, behavioral rating, and civic alignment are silently shared.

Officially, this system exists to ensure safety and transparency.
In practice, it reinforces conformity through constant social surveillance and creates the illusion that everyone supports the system.

### 2.4 Social Structure Inside the Elite Cities
Tier 1 — The Owners  
Corporate dynasties and power brokers. They control systems.

Tier 2 — The Managers  
Executives, strategists, and cultural architects who guide AI behavior and narratives.

Tier 3 — The Specialists  
Engineers, doctors, scientists, and legal experts. Wealthy, but replaceable.

Tier 4 — The Serviced Class  
Artists, influencers, and luxury service workers who produce identity, meaning, and status.

Everyone is comfortable.
Power is not equal.


### 2.5 Life Outside the Elite Cities
Outside the cities lie abandoned zones and decaying infrastructure.
Those without valid chips are cut off from all automated systems.

They lack access to:
• Internet  
• Transportation  
• Healthcare  
• Corporate supply chains  

Survival depends on manual labor, trade, scavenging, and stolen technology.

Underground networks and resistance cells slowly form.

### 2.6 The Moon Datacenters (2300)
Earth can no longer sustain the environmental cost of large-scale computation. The solution is irreversible: datacenters are relocated to the Moon.

The Moon becomes the digital brain of Elite Cities:
• AI governance  
• Surveillance logic  
• Behavioral prediction  
• Resource allocation

Most humans around this time are entirely shaped by the system.
The Identity Chip, seamless convenience, and the absence of any awareness of life beyond the Elite City have diminished essential human traits.

## 2.7. The Far Future (post 2500)

### 2.7.1 Inside The Elite Cities - Numb Era 

The Elite Cities become fully self regulating environments where human behavior is continuously optimized.
The Numb era happens gradually, as each generation inherits a world where everything works. Human experience narrows into safe, repeatable patterns. Curiosity fades. Doubt becomes biologically uncomfortable. Over time, people do not lose their freedom, they lose their understanding of what it means to be human.

The Identity Chip remains a foundation of the system, but the deeper change is cultural and cognitive.
What was once social control becomes neurological alignment.

CLARIFICATION : Humans are not controlled. They are numb. They can change their behavior if they want, they just don't know that this is even an option.

### 2.7.2 Outside The Elite Cities

Outside the system, and hidden within its edges, a small group remains.

Some are former citizens of the Elite Cities, engineers, scientists, and operators who recognized the trajectory early and chose to leave.
Their escape was neither simple nor safe. Removing the Identity Chip was possible, but difficult, often incomplete, and always irreversible.

Others have never belonged to the system at all.
They are descendants of those who were excluded from the beginning, people who lived and survived beyond the reach of the cities.

Together, they form a fragmented but persistent population and are among the last individuals capable of understanding that something has to change.

Their goal is not to destroy the system, but to ***reverse its effects from within the human mind.***

#### 2.7.2.1 The Discovery — The Moon Archives

At some point, an opportunity emerges outside the elite cities.

A fragmented backup is recovered from the lunar datacenters — a preserved dataset from an earlier stage of Elite Cities, some years after the id chip was introduced.

Within it, they gain access to the raw data collected through the Identity Chip — a complete record of human behavior, captured over years to refine and train the system’s AI.
This had long been suspected. Now, it is confirmed. The id chip collected personal data. 

It contains:
• Personal conversations
• Behavioral logs
• Emotional patterns
• Decision-making traces
• Unfiltered human interaction

#### 2.7.2.2 The Simulation Project

Using this data, the group begins constructing a machine learning model.

Combining:
• Archived behavioral data ***of a citizen from the backup***
• Advanced AI modeling
• Neuroscience and brain-interface knowledge

They create a full simulation of ***one's citizen mind as it once was.***

Inside the simulation, the player will step directly into their reconstructed consciousness, exploring memories, conversations, and thoughts as the simulation takes place.
The goal is to experiment, to understand which stimuli can break through the numbness that dominates life inside the current system, find what is missing.
